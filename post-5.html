How Long Should a Laptop Battery Actually Last

Laptops · June 21, 2026

A laptop battery rated for 600 charge cycles doesn't die at cycle 601 — it just drifts further from its original capacity. By the two-year mark, most laptops are running at 80-85% of day-one battery life, and that's normal wear, not a defect.

Heat is the real enemy, more than charge cycles. A laptop that spends its life plugged in on a warm desk degrades faster than one that gets unplugged and carried around, because heat breaks down the battery chemistry over time.

Most modern laptops have a battery health setting buried in the power menu that caps charging at 80% for daily use. Turning it on costs you a bit of runtime per charge, but it meaningfully slows down long-term degradation if the laptop spends most of its life plugged in.

When a battery's capacity drops below roughly 60% of original, that's the point where most people notice real impact on their day — that's the signal to start budgeting for a replacement, not a panic point.
