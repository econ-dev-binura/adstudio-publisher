The Laptop Buying Guide Nobody Asked For

Tech · June 18, 2026

Every year laptops get thinner, faster, and somehow more confusing to shop for. Marketing copy throws around terms like "AI-ready" and "all-day battery" without telling you what that means for the way you actually use a computer.

If you're mostly browsing, writing, and streaming video, you don't need the flagship chip. Mid-range processors from the last two generations handle that workload fine, and you'll save real money skipping the halo model.

Advertisement

Battery life claims are measured under ideal conditions, so treat the advertised number as a ceiling, not a guarantee. Real-world use with a bright screen and a dozen browser tabs will usually cut that number by a third.

Storage is the one spec worth not skimping on. A 256GB drive fills up fast once you factor in OS updates, photos, and a couple of games. Going up to 512GB is usually the better trade than spending the difference on a faster CPU.
